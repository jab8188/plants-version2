Care

Here well go over how to care for some of your very own plants in your home.

  • String of hearts

    String of hearts is one plant that loves to be in a hanging basket, as the vines grow you can coil them up into the basket to make it appear fuller and give your plant room to grow. These plants love bright light and not a lot of water, primarily when dry. Fun fact: when you can fold the leaves into a taco they need water.

    String of hearts

  • Snake Plants

    Snake plants are very easy to care for. They love any light you give them, loves bright light but will survive in low light areas in your home as well. These ones you want to water when dry. Fun fact: The snake plant is considered a weed in parts of Australia.

    Snake plants

  • Spider plants

    Spider plants are a very popular beginners starter plant. Even with some neglect in the beginning this plant will still survive for a short time, no need for a lot of water but still enough where the soil is wet, not damp. This plant looks like a resemblance of a spide. They form clusters on the ends of the leaves, in which you use these babies as a way to grow more; also known as, propagating.

    Spider plants

  • Purple Passion

    Purple passion plant is one of many house plants that has a beautiful appearance. These plants like bright light but can tolerate medium light. Very easy one to care for, even for those who are still figuring out their regimen to grow these plants. Fun fact: this plant will help puirify the air in your home.

    Purple passion

  • Mother of Millions

    Mother of millions or Mother of thousands prefer bright light and are native to Madagascar. This plant is unusual in its own way, the "babies" of these plants grow around the edge of each leaf and drops them to the ground. Where the little ones drop is where they will grow. Some areas find this plant a nusance due to its rapid growth, especially in prime areas.

    Mother of Millions

  • Bonsai

    Bonsai trees started over 2000 years ago in China, but what we know as bonsai today was developed by Japanese. These trees are definitly one of patience for growth, but you get to train the branches where you want them to grow. These love water and light.

    Bonsai

Hope these tips and tricks helped you!
